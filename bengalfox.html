Bengal Fox

Also known as Indian Fox

Brief Description About Bengal Fox

The Bengal Fox is a kind of endemic Asian foxes for the Indian subcontinent. Its large, thick and black tail is its most important feature, which represents up to 60% of your body's length. Usually, the queue queues behind, but when the fox is running, takes the tail horizontally, then it takes vertically doing sudden revs. The color of the Fox layer varies according to the season and inside a population, but it is usually gray on the back and pale in the belly, with dark brown on the ears, with black edges. Their ears are great compared to their body and are possibly an adaptation for thermoregulation in their hot and dry habitats.

Behavior and Ecology

During the heat of the day, bengal fox hide under vegetation or in subterranean dens that they dig. The dens are usually huge and complex with numerous chambers and escape routes. They are sometimes seen basking at a vantage point around sunrise or sunset. Their lifespan is about 6 to 8 years.

Diet

The Bengal fox feeds on rodents, reptiles, crabs, termites, insects, small birds, and fruits. Scats of young pups appeared to show that they fed mainly on rodents but are opportunistic feeders.

Communication

Foxes make a wide range of vocalizations. A chattering cry is the most common call. They also growl, whine, whimper, and bark. They can be heard howling in the night in groups.
